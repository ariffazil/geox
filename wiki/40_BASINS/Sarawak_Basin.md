## Overview

The **Sarawak Basin** is a Tertiary sedimentary basin located offshore northwest Borneo, Malaysia. It is one of Southeast Asia's most prolific hydrocarbon provinces, with cumulative production exceeding **15 billion barrels of oil equivalent**.

| Attribute | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| **Location** | 2°N–6°N, 108°E–114°E |
| **Area** | ~150,000 km² (offshore) |
| **Water Depth** | 20–2,000 m |
| **Basin Type** | Passive margin, deltaic to deepwater |
| **Age** | Eocene–Recent |
| **Total Sediment Thickness** | Up to 12 km |
| **Discovered Fields** | 120+ |
| **Cumulative Production** | >15 BBOE |

## Regional Setting

### Tectonic Framework

The Sarawak Basin formed in response to:
1. **Eocene–Oligocene:** Opening of South China Sea
2. **Miocene:** Collision of India with Eurasia (distant effect)
3. **Pliocene–Recent:** Regional subsidence and sediment loading

## Play Types

### 1. Miocene Deltaic (Shelf)
- **Setting:** Sarawak Shelf, <200m water
- **Reservoir:** Cycle IV–V deltaic sandstones
- **Trap:** Structural (rollover anticlines)
- **Risk:** Low–Moderate
- **Status:** Mature, infill drilling

### 2. Luconia Carbonate Platform
- **Setting:** 200–1,000m water
- **Reservoir:** Miocene carbonate buildups
- **Trap:** Stratigraphic (reef buildups)
- **Risk:** Moderate
- **Status:** Active exploration

### 3. Deepwater Fold Belt Turbidites
- **Setting:** >1,000m water
- **Reservoir:** Pliocene turbidite fans
- **Trap:** Structural (fold-thrust anticlines)
- **Risk:** High
- **Status:** Emerging play

### 4. Sub-Thrust
- **Setting:** Deepwater fold belt, >2,000m burial
- **Reservoir:** Miocene sandstones
- **Trap:** Structural (sub-thrust closures)
- **Risk:** Very High
- **Status:** Frontier
